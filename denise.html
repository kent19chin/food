A little bit about
Denise Harrison

Denise Harrison is an English Professor at Kent State University.
She shares her knowledge of food with her students. She has
ten siblings and is a mother of four who loves cooking
and discovering new recipes.

A Story in the Past

Baked Beans with Pineapple

When Denise was young, her mom cooked most of the family meals. Many of these meals contained lots of beans, which is why now, Denise would prefer not to eat beans if she could. However, when Denise does make beans, she would make baked beans with pineapple, the exact same recipe as her late mother. She would find comfort in occasionally cooking the same meals as her mother did.

Mac and Cheese

When Denise was growing up, her family mainly ate african american cooking. Traditionally, this meant lots of salt, fat, and sugar. An example of this is macaroni and cheese, which is a relatively simple dish but with all the common additions, such as multiple cheeses, butter, pork and bacon, it becomes a lot more unhealthy. Denise now makes mac and cheese by replacing some ingredients so it can be healthy and delicious.

Present Day Story

Denise has changed her diet after going to graduate school and starting her own family. She tried to cook healthier and only ate organic food. After watching documentaries about healthy eating and people around her went vegan, Denise decided to become vegan as well. Denise calls herself a flexible vegan because she would eat meat and use butter in her meal occasionally. In her current diet, she tries to buy everything organic, make more soup, and avoids canned food.

A Hope for the Future

During the time we are facing the COVID-19 disease, Denise and her husband are not able to see some of their family members and enjoy a wonderful dinner with them at Christmas. After a few interviews with Denise, we found that she loves cooking a lot. We film this short video, trying to stand on her view. In the video, we cook “soul food” and taste it, to indicate the picture when Denise cooks her family’s favorite food for her family members and enjoy the meal together without any restriction in the future.
